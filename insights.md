# Insights from Education Data Analysis

## Observations

• Public schools are unevenly distributed across cities, with some cities having significantly higher concentrations.

• District-level spending varies widely, with some districts spending substantially more per pupil.

• Higher per-pupil spending does not always correspond with higher graduation rates.

• Districts with higher percentages of teachers rated "exemplary" appear more frequently among higher-performing graduation outcomes, suggesting a potential relationship worth deeper statistical analysis.
